# LH FRMS — Rev 4.1 → Rev 5 Change Summary (A380/A330/B787)

Document: A380/A330/B787 FRMS Ruleset Revision 5, Issue Date: 15 June 2026  
Scope: Chapters 1A (Planning) and 1B (Operational)

---

## Chapter 1A — Planning

### FD3.1 — 2-Pilot Planning Table

**Flight time limit column removed entirely.** Rev 4.1 showed explicit flight time limits (8 hrs, 8.5 hrs, 9.5 hrs by start band). Rev 5 removes these — no flight time limits column exists for 2-pilot planning.

Duty period limits and sign-on bands are **unchanged**:
- 0500–0759: 11 hrs
- 0800–1359: 11 hrs (or 12 hrs for 1-day pattern only, max 4 sectors)
- 1400–1559: 11 hrs
- 1600–0459: 10 hrs

Sector limits unchanged. Pre/post duty rest tables unchanged.

---

### FD3.1 — 3-Pilot Planning Table

**Flight time limit column removed** (was 8.5 hrs for Class 2, 12.5 hrs for Class 1).

Duty period limits unchanged:
- Class 2 Rest: 12 hrs
- Class 1 Rest: 14 hrs

Sector limits unchanged (3 if duty >11, otherwise max 4). Pre/post duty rest tables unchanged.

---

### FD3.1 — 4-Pilot Planning Table

No change in duty period limits or rest tables. Inflight management column retained (8 hrs continuous / 14 hrs total flight deck).

---

### FD3.2.1(c) — Pilot Discretion for Pre-Duty Rest Reduction

**Removed.** Rev 4.1 stated: "If the pilot considers themselves physically and mentally fit for duty, the above pre-duty rest may be reduced to the minimum applicable under the Operational Limits in Chapter 1B." This provision is gone in Rev 5 — planning rest can no longer be self-reduced to operational limits outside of a disruption context.

---

### FD3.2.2 — Crew Rest Aircraft Types

Class 2 rest aircraft updated: A330-200 **ALL** dedicated crew rest area at seat 5A → A330-200**L** dedicated crew rest area at seat 5A. Narrows scope from all A330-200 variants to the L variant only.

Class 1 rest aircraft (A380-800, B787-9, A330-300 International, A330-200L International mid-cabin) — **unchanged**.

---

### FD3.4 — Relevant Sectors (Patterns >18 hours)

**New sector added: Perth to Paris and vice versa** (item f).

Rev 4.1 listed a–e:
- Any planned duty period >18 hours
- Sydney to Dallas and vice versa
- Melbourne to Dallas and vice versa
- Perth to London and vice versa
- Auckland to New York and vice versa

Rev 5 adds: **(f) Perth to Paris and vice versa**

---

## Chapter 1B — Operational

### FD10.1 — 2-Pilot Operational Table

**Flight time limits removed.** Rev 4.1 had three rows for ALL sign-on times showing 9.5 hrs / 10 hrs / 10.5 hrs flight time limits with conditions. Rev 5 removes these entirely. Only duty period limit of **12 hrs** remains (unchanged).

Pre-duty and post-duty rest values **unchanged**.

---

### FD10.1 — 3-Pilot Operational Table

Duty period limits **unchanged**:
- Seat in Passenger Compartment: 14 hrs
- Class 2 Rest: 16 hrs
- Class 1 Rest: 18 hrs

Inflight management text retained (max 8 continuous hrs on flight deck, max 14 hrs total flight deck). Sector limit (≤2 if duty >14 hrs) unchanged. Pre/post duty rest unchanged.

---

### FD10.1 — 4-Pilot Operational Table

**Two new crew rest facility combinations added:**

| Crew Rest Facility | Rev 4.1 | Rev 5 |
|---|---|---|
| Seats in Passenger Compartment | 14 hrs | 14 hrs |
| **1 x Class 2 REST & 1 x Seat in Pax Compartment** | Not present | **16 hrs — NEW** |
| 2 x Class 2 REST | 16 hrs | 16 hrs |
| **1 x Class 1 REST & 1 x Seat in Pax Compartment** | Not present | **18 hrs — NEW** |
| 1 x Class 1 & 1 x Class 2 REST | 20 hrs | 20 hrs |
| 2 x Class 1 REST | 20 hrs | 20 hrs |
| 2 x Class 1 REST (Relevant Sectors per FD10.4) | 21 hrs (A380 & B787 only) | 21 hrs (A380 & B787 only) |

Note *1 (mixed facilities, priority to higher class for landing crew) retained.

---

### FD10.2 — Application of Flight Duty Time Tables (Operational)

**New section.** Provides duty limit considerations guidance — flight must not commence take-off if apparent it cannot be completed within limits. Guidance on fastest flight time, ATC holding, INTER/TEMPO weather, en-route factors, diversion assessment. Was not in our operational HTML.

---

### FD10.3.1 — Rest Requirements (Post-Disruption)

Restructured and clarified. Minimum rest following a disruption after pattern commencement shall be:
- (i) Rest per Operational Limits in Chapter 1B or at pilot discretion, provided it does not exceed the hourly amount of applicable rest limitations
- (ii) 10 hours; or hours of previous duty (max 12) + 1.5× time over 12 hrs + timezone difference over 3 hrs; or
- (iii) 24 hours pre and post duty where duty was planned or was planned for greater than 16 hours

---

### FD10.4 — Relevant Sectors Disruption Limits (Operational)

**Moved from Planning chapter (was FD3.4.4 in Rev 4.1) into Operational chapter as FD10.4.**

Same Relevant Sectors list as FD3.4 including new **Perth to Paris**.

Post-relevant sector disruption rest limits:
- Prior to operating a Relevant Sector: 22 hours (unchanged)
- After operating a Relevant Sector:
  - (i) Captain OR First Officer: 27 hours
  - (ii) Captain OR F/O and duty >20 hours: 36 hours
  - (iii) Captain AND First Officer: 36 hours
  - **(iv) Second Officer(s): 27 hours — NEW**
  - (v) If Duty Period <18 hours: Chapter 1B limits apply per FD10.1
  - (vi) If Duty Period >18 hours, pilot fit, next sector flight time <4 hours: 24 hours (min rest before next Relevant Sector then 36 hours)
- After Relevant Sector inbound to Australia or New Zealand:
  - (i) Next operation to same time zone destination: 36 hours
  - (ii) Next operation is domestic or trans-Tasman: 22 hours

---

### FD6 — Cumulative Limitations (Planning)

FD6.1 — clarified that the 50-hour duty count **explicitly includes Standbys**. Same 24-hour rest requirement after 50 hours.

FD6.2 — total flight time in any consecutive 28 days: **100 hours** (unchanged).

FD6.3 — roster promulgation limit: **950 hours** in any 365 consecutive days (unchanged).

FD6.4.2 — 7-day cumulative duty: **60 hours** (unchanged).

FD6.4.3 — 14-day cumulative duty: **100 hours** (unchanged, but now stated explicitly as 100 hrs rather than derived from the 14-day rule).

FD6.6.3(b) — Mixed crew 2-pilot 7-day rule updated: pilot operating entirely as 2-pilot crew must have one period of free duty starting no later than **22:00 local**, finishing not earlier than **05:00 local** in any consecutive **8 nights** (previously less specific).
