  ---
  What the HTML contains that is NOT in the SH models

  The models only cover FD13 (planning) and FD23 (operational) — the duty/flight time limits. Everything else in the HTML is absent from
  both model files:

  Cumulative limits (FD11–12 / FD21–22):
  - Planning has a 13 consecutive bid periods: 950 hr flight time limit (FD11.1) — not tracked anywhere in the project
  - The 14-day duty limit has two values: 90 hrs (initial roster publication) vs 100 hrs (with pilot agreement or open time bid).
  FRMSFleet.maxDutyTime14Days uses only 100 — the 90 hr initial threshold is never surfaced
  - 1.5× simulator/training factor for cumulative duty (FD12.1 / FD22.1) — not modeled
  - Max 9 days in any 11-day period; max 6 consecutive duty days (FD12.2 / FD22.2) — tracked in FRMSCumulativeTotals but the rule
  definition is not in the SH model files
  - Min 36 hrs free of all duty in any 7 consecutive days (FD12.3 / FD22.3) — not in SH models

  Rules entirely absent from the SH models:
  - FD13.2 / FD23.2 — Emergency Procedures Training extension to 15 hrs for non-SYD/MEL based pilots
  - FD13.5 / FD23.5 — Reserve duty max 12 consecutive hours
  - FD13.6 — Consecutive early starts: max 4 consecutive duties with sign-on prior to 0706 (definition not in SH_Planning_FltDuty;
  computed elsewhere)
  - FD14 / FD24 — Late night operations full ruleset:
    - Max 4 consecutive nights; 5 nights once per 28 days exception
    - FD14.3(a): Max 40 hrs duty in any 7-night period when >2 LNO duties
    - FD14.3(b): Max 4 duty periods in that 7-night period
    - FD14.3(c): Recovery — remain on LNO or min 24 hrs free before any other duty
  - FD14.4 — Back of clock (planning HTML only — see discrepancy below)
  - FD15 / FD25 — Deadheading rules (6 clauses, including the 16 hr absolute maximum for any duty with flight duty)
  - FD17 (planning) — Split duty: min 6 hrs rest, +4 hrs from FD13.1, max 16 hrs total; 50% rest discount (max 4 hrs); 2300–0530
  restriction
  - FD27 (operational) — Split duty: two accommodation types — sleeping (+4 hrs, 16 hr max) vs resting (+2 hrs, no stated max); 2300–0530
   rule
  - FD18.1 / FD28 — Time free within a pattern: 10 hrs or equal to duty (whichever greater) when duty ≤12; 12 + 1.5×(duty−12) when duty
  >12
  - FD28 additionally: rest may be reduced to 9 hrs when duty ≤10 hrs and rest includes 2200–0600; standby with no callout → 10 hrs
  minimum
  - FD19 — Time free following a pattern: 12 hrs (1–2 day pattern); 15 hrs (3–4 day pattern)
  - FD20 / FD28.5 — Days free: 7 days in any 28 days and 24 days in any 84 days; 8 days per calendar month; 26 days in any 3 consecutive
  calendar months

  ---
  Discrepancies (values/rules in the models that conflict with HTML)

  1. Back-of-clock (FD14.4) — Planning only
  The back-of-clock restriction (sign-on no earlier than 1000 LT next day) appears only in the planning HTML (FD14.4). It is absent from
  the operational HTML entirely. However, SH_NextDutyView shows it as an "Active Restriction" without distinguishing between planning and
   operational modes. Per the HTML source of truth, it should not appear when viewing operational limits.

  2. Planning flight time UI — simplified incorrectly
  SH_NextDutyView shows planning flight time as two columns: Standard = 10.0 hrs and > 7 hrs Night = 9.5 hrs. But the planning HTML
  (FD13.3) specifies the same three conditions as operational:
  7 hrs darkness → 9.5 hrs
  1 sector → 10.0 hrs
  - All other occasions → 10.5 hrs

  The SH_Planning_FltDuty model has all three values correctly. The UI drops the 10.5 hr single-sector case and mislabels 10.0 as
  "Standard." It should display 3 columns, identical to the operational view.
