Reserves FD23.3–23.5
FD23.3 — Reserve duty max 12 consecutive hours. Access to suitable sleeping accommodation and free from all duties associated with employment.
FD23.4 — If called out from reserve, max combined duration of reserve + subsequent duty period is 16 hours, except where (a) it is an augmented crew operation; (b) split duty per FD27; or (c) when operationally necessary to complete the objective of the duty and the pilot considers themselves physically and mentally fit — max combined duration 18 hours.
FD23.5 — If a reserve starts between 2300–0600, that period does not count toward the FD23.4 combined limit until the crew member is contacted by the Company.
Late Night Ops, Back of Clock & Early Starts FD24
FD24.1 — After more than 2 consecutive LNO flying duties, a 24-hour free-of-duty period is required before any other flying duty.
FD24.2 — Max 4 LNO flying duty periods in any 168 hours.
FD24.3 — FD24.1 and FD24.2 do not apply to reserve duty periods.
FD24.4 — Max 2 BOC flying duty periods in any 168 hours (pilot may agree to waive).
FD24.5 — After a BOC flying duty, the next flying duty in Australia is limited to sign-on no earlier than 1000 local the following day (pilot may agree to waive).
Deadheading Following a Flight Duty FD25
FD25.1 — May deadhead following flight duty up to Operational Limits (FD23.1 table).
FD25.2 — At pilot discretion, FD23.1 limits may be extended when deadheading.
FD25.3 — Deadheading included in total duty period for rest and FDT calculations.
FD25.4 — Deadheading sector (if last sector) does not count in sector determination for max duty.
FD25.5 — Deadheading sector before a flight duty is counted as a sector.
FD25.6 — No duty period with flight duty to exceed 16 hours.

FD27.1 — Split duty only where no reasonable alternative is available and pilot consents.

FD27.4 — If rest includes any period between 2300–0530: rest must be uninterrupted min 7 hrs at suitable sleeping accommodation; max FDP may be increased to 16 hrs (if not already permitted); rest discounting not permitted.
