Reserves FD13.3–13.5
FD13.3 — Reserve duty max 12 consecutive hours. Access to suitable sleeping accommodation and free from all duties associated with employment.
FD13.4 — If called out from reserve, max combined duration of reserve + subsequent duty period is 16 hours, except where (a) it is an augmented crew operation, or (b) split duty per FD17.
FD13.5 — If a reserve starts between 2300–0600, that period does not count toward the FD13.4 combined limit until the crew member is contacted by the Company.
Late Night Ops, Back of Clock & Early Starts FD14
FD14.1 — After more than 2 consecutive LNO flying duties, a 24-hour free-of-duty period is required before any other flying duty.
FD14.2 — Max 4 LNO flying duty periods in any 168 hours.
FD14.3 — FD14.1 and FD14.2 do not apply to reserve duty periods.
FD14.4 — Max 2 BOC flying duty periods in any 168 hours (pilot may agree to waive).
FD14.5 — After a BOC flying duty, the next flying duty in Australia is limited to sign-on no earlier than 1000 local the following day (pilot may agree to waive).
FD14.6 — Maximum 4 consecutive duties with sign-on prior to 0706 local time.
Deadheading Following a Flight Duty FD15
FD15.1 — May deadhead following flight duty up to Operational Limits (FD23.1 table).
FD15.2 — At pilot discretion, FD23.1 limits may be extended when deadheading.
FD15.3 — Deadheading included in total duty period for rest and FDT calculations.
FD15.4 — Deadheading sector (if last sector) does not count in sector determination for max duty.
FD15.5 — Deadheading sector before a flight duty is counted as a sector.
FD15.6 — No duty period with flight duty to exceed 16 hours.
